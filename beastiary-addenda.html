The Witcher 3 Enhanced Edition Help

Bestiary Addenda

Humans

We all know humans are the real monsters.

Humans can be directly executed when low on poise (use kicks), but this leaves you vulnerable to attacks.

A good trick is to cast an alt Aard when an enemy is low on poise in a group, then use the stagger time to execute them.

The "backward" execute (click + backwards) is quicker and leaves you facing anyone approaching from behind.

Alternatively, just hammer down their health with rends.

Ranged enemies tend to be the most dangerous by far. You can reflect their bolts back with a bit of practice, but in a hectic fight, it is best to stun them via Axii or take them down first.

Necrophages

I believe everyone's introduction to W3EE is the pain of fighting a group of Drowners unprepared.

Avoid group fights using Axii, bombs etc. whenever possible. Single Drowners, Foglets and hags can be killed quickly via poise break + Aard.

Wolves, Dogs, Nekkers and small Insectoids

These all have very quick attacks that can hurt a lot when not blocked, and wolves and dogs also dodge a lot.

Stuns and staggers are your friend.

Keep your guard up until you can isolate a single enemy, then finish them quickly by chaining kicks and heavies.

Flyers (draconids, griffins)

The best tip I can give for these is that the wing attacks are hard to dodge, so you should not rely on attacking them from behind too much. I have found it safer to spam Igni after drinking a Maribor forest, land a few hits when you know it's safe, and get out again. Of course, liberal use of Broadhead bolts and poison also helps.

Wyverns and related creatures have a different behavior quick attacks that can mostly be blocked or parried, so a defensive style works better than dodging around.

Spectres

Noonwraiths & Nightwraiths are IMO among the easiest foes in the game: Just make sure you stay inside a Yrden sign, and Counterattack → Heavy Attack every time they approach.

Wraiths

Wraiths can be extremely nasty, and I am still torn between two approaches:

  • Either stay in a Quen counter all their attacks, or

  • Roll away as soon as they teleport behind you.

Both approaches heavily rely on getting the timing right, and you are very screwed as soon as they manage to hit you, so I would recommend a blizzard potion.

Bears and Fiends

Bears have big health bars and very quick short range attacks that can stun you through blocks. Run or bend past them, attack from behind, and retreat or use a sign when low on stamina. Note that their turn-around counter-attacks can be safely blocked.

Fiends and Chorts are similar to bears, but more. Huge health bars, insane regeneration, and devastate you when you are in front of them. Dish out as much poison and bleed damage as you can from behind with a dodge/bend attack pattern. Broadhead bolts can help to keep the bleeding up when you are low on stamina. Their attempts to hypnotize you offer an opportunity for at least one safe attack.

Werewolves and Lesser Vampires

Not too high in health, but unrelenting, quick and nasty attacks. Will react to many hits with a quick counter-attack that is impossible to dodge, so Blizzard and Tiara are both very useful.

Longer stuns that gives you time to breathe, be it via Igni, or a bomb, are very valuable.

Leshens

A single dimeritium bomb will prevent a Leshen from calling wolves and teleporting, making it an excellent investment. Without the teleport, its attacks are deadly, but predictable.

Either counter-attack ( Quen and Tiara reduce damage through block), out-range them with good rend timing, or dodge around and attack from behind.

The Hymn is a re-skinned Leshen AFAIK.

Golems & Gargoyles

Ah, the bane of W3EE.

These suck, even for experienced players. Attacking a Golem really does not require a lot of finesse, and Armor piercing (or reduction) is very helpful.

Be sure to use rend attacks though, they are quicker and more effective than regular heavies. Otherwise, you can fall back on alt Yrden amplified by at least one Dimeritium bomb (riving blast), as well as the ever-useful grapeshot.

The best safe pattern I have found is a very mechanical:

Rend ⭢ Jump Away ⭢ ⮌ Repeat

The two-handed Lucerne Battle Hammer you can buy from every smith works surprisingly well with this tactic.

Attacks & Abilities

Stomp Attack

Their stomp attack hits you for heavy damage even when you dodge, so be ready to roll away after every attack and Quen up for safety.

Alternatively, with some dodge speed, practice and preferably Blizzard or Cat, you can roll (away + to the side) at the very last second.

If you are close enough, Parrying at the perfect moment can avoid damage altogether. (This may be a bug)

Charge Attack

It has a cooldown and will trigger when the Golem looks directly at you, so you can bait it out from a safe distance and then run away, preferably behind an obstacle.

However, if it starts its charge while you are in an Attack animation, you are screwed, so try to avoid going in with Long-Distance Attacks and instead walk up to them.

Acid Spray

This ability is often extremely frustrating to deal with when not handled properly. Gargoyles can finish off an Attack-chain with this, inflicting a strong poison on Geralt.

Roll out of the way as soon as possible to avoid this, and avoid Attacking the Gargoyles backside too closely while it is spraying, as the Hit-box may still apply the poison from behind

Quen can also block the poison from being applied.
